FIREARM TRANSFER AND SALE REQUIREMENTS:

Below you will find the required information you will need to purchase a firearm or initiate a transfer through us. Please contact us if you have any questions.

You must be 18 to purchase Rifles & Non-Pistol Grip Shotguns
You must be 21 to purchase Handguns, Stripped Lower Receivers & Pistol Grip Shotguns

LONG GUNS:

• Valid California License or Identification Card with current residential address
• Secondary Proof of Residency if your ID does not have your current residential address - MUST be Government-issued (See Government-Issued Documentation requirements below).
• Valid FSC (Firearm Safety Certificate). See below for more information.
• US Citizen (Legal Alien or Nonimmigrant Alien, see below).

HANDGUNS:

• Valid California Driver's License or Identification Card with Current address.
• Secondary Proof of Residency (Hard-wired Utility bill dated within the last 90 days, Residential Lease, Property Deed, Current Government-issued License/ Permit/ Registration). NOTE: If your identification does not have your current address, your Secondary Proof must be Government-issued. See below for Government issued Documentation requirements).
• Valid FSC (Firearm Safety Certificate) or HSC (Handgun Safety Certificate). See below for more information.
• US Citizen (Legal Alien or Nonimmigrant Alien, see below).

LEGAL ALIEN/ NONIMMIGRANT ALIENS:

Legal Aliens and Nonimmigrant Aliens must provide their current Resident Alien/ I-9 Card in addition to the requirements listed above.

DROS (Dealer Record of Sale):

All DROS have a mandatory 10-day waiting period (10 x 24 hour periods or 240 hours) unless you have a valid waiting period exemption.  We are not able to release your firearm until the full waiting period has passed.  We require an 11-day waiting period to ensure that the full 240 hours is met.  In most circumstances we can release on the 10th day so long as you know what time you DROSed the gun and arrange to make your pickup after that time. All firearms must be picked up within 30 days of the initial DROS date.  If you fail to pick up your firearm within the 30 days, your DROS is automatically cancelled and you will have to re-start the DROS process.  Fees will apply if a re-DROS is required.

ALL FIREARM SALES ARE FINAL

If you decide to cancel your purchase, we charge a restocking fee of 25% of the retail price plus any applicable DOJ fees.  A "DENIED" DROS response is charged a 50% restocking fee plus any applicable DOJ fees.

STORAGE FEES/ ABANDONED FIREARMS:

We are pretty flexible when it comes to storage fees.  If you are not able to come in to start your DROS immediately, please just let us know as soon as possible when you think you will be able to come in.  For guns that are received in error or guns that are not on the CA Approved Roster of Handguns, we charge a $75 fee for return shipping and paperwork fees to return the gun to the seller.  If we notify you that a gun needs to be returned for any reason and you do not respond or refuse to pay the return fees, after 90 days the gun will be considered abandoned and will be sold to recoup fees or turned over to local law enforcement for destruction, at our discretion.

FIREARM SAFETY CERTIFICATE (FSC):

**Effective January 1st, 2015**

We offer the FSC test at our location.  Cost is $25 - $15 paid to the State and $10 dealer fee.

This is a 30 question test and is required in order to purchase any handgun or long gun in the state of California as of January 1, 2015. 

The FSC Study guide can be found here:  http://oag.ca.gov/sites/all/files/agweb/pdfs/firearms/forms/hscsg.pdf?

If you have questions about the FSC Program, their FAQ can be found here: http://oag.ca.gov/firearms/fscfaqs

You may be exempt from the FSC requirement if you meet any of the following criteria:
• Special Weapons Permit holder
• Operation of Law Representative
• Handgun being returned to the Owner
• FFL Collector with COE (curio and relic handguns only)
• Military - Active Duty
• Military - Reserve
• Military - Honorably Retired
• Peace Officer - California - Active
• Peace Officer - Federal - Active
• Peace Officer - California - Honorably Retired
• Peace Officer - California - Reserve
• Peace Officer - Federal - Honorably Retired
• Carry Concealed Weapon (CCW) Permit Holder
• P.O.S.T. 832 PC (Firearms) Training
• Particular and Limited Authority Peace Officers
• Law Enforcement Service Gun to Family Member
• Valid Hunting License (long guns only)

FIREARM SAFETY DEVICES:

Effective January 1, 2002, the State of California requires that all firearms sold within the state, whether new or used, must be accompanied by a certified CA Department of Justice firearm safety device.  Most new guns come with a cable lock; however, locks are required for all guns, new or used, including lower receivers.  We have locks available for purchase for $10.00 + tax or you may purchase a lock elsewhere and provide us with a receipt dated within the last 30 days for our records. The CA DOJ Approved Firearm Safety Device list can be found here:  http://oag.ca.gov/firearms/fsdcertlist/

If you have an approved gun safe, you are exempt from the gun lock requirement for long guns only.  You can fill out and sign a Safe Affidavit to include with your file to meet this requirement. You must know your gun safe make and model and safety features for the Safe Affidavit.

GOVERNMENT ISSUED SECONDARY PROOF OF RESIDENCY REQUIREMENTS:

If your CDL/ ID does not have your current residential address, you must provide an additional Government-Issued Document to meet the proof of residency requirements.  This document must include:
• Issuing Government Agency's Information
• Be valid: not expired or before its period of validity
• Buyer's Full Name (Must match ID and have any suffixes: Jr., Sr., II, III, etc.)
• Buyer's Current Residential address
• Be printed by the government agency that issued it and mailed to you
• Be printed out in a government office if picked up in person

Examples of some approved documents:
• DMV Vehicle Registration                                                    • DMV Driving Record print out
• DMV Boat/ Trailer/ Bike Registration                                  • License/ Permit (Government Issued)
• Tax Bill                                                                                  • Pilots License
• Guard Card/ Weapons Card                                                   • Voter Registration/ Ballots/ Documents
• Tax Return Check                                                                  • Handicap Registry
• Jury Summons                                                                        • Certificate of Title from DMV
• Property Deed from County                                                   • Court Subpoena
• Government issued check                                                      • Non DMV Issued Government ID
• Unemployment check

Unacceptable Documents for Secondary Proof of Address include:
• DMV Change of Address cards                                             • DMV Website change of address print outs
• Hunting/ Fishing License                                                       • Utility Bill (Unless issued by City/ County)
• Passport                                                                                  • Credit card bills
• Cell Phone Bill                                                                       • Bank statements
• Insurance Bills                                                                        • Mortgage Statements/ Bills
• Loan Statements/ Bills                                                            • Speeding Ticket
