Las Ventanas’ West Side model an award winner

By Tania Soussan / For the Journal
Published: Sunday, November 16th, 2014 at 12:02am
Updated: Monday, November 17th, 2014 at 8:37am
.......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... ..........

The dining room and kitchen of this 2,146-square-foot home feature ceramic tiles that give the appearance of distressed barn wood — a crowd pleaser among Parade of Homes visitors. (Adolphe Pierre-Louis/Albuquerque Journal)

As visitors walk in the front door of Las Ventanas Homes’ award-winning model at Ventana de la Luz, chances are they will ooh and ahh over the flooring.

The 9-by-36—inch ceramic tiles with a tight grout line give the appearance of distressed barn wood, and the result is a beautiful and durable floor unlikely to show much dirt.

The floor earned the most compliments from visitors during the fall Parade of Homes, but plenty of other features won praise as well, including the open great room and the California Closets system in the master suite.

A gated courtyard marks the entrance to this Build Green New Mexico model by Las Ventanas. (Adolphe Pierre-Louis/Albuquerque Journal)

In fact, the model won all four awards in its price category during the Parade — best kitchen and best bathroom as well as the premier award and premier green award. Building professionals act as the judges for those prizes.

“They did it right. It’s a beautiful property,” said John Garcia, executive vice president of the Home Builders Association of Central New Mexico.

Ventana del la Luz, a 30-lot community on the last of the available land in the 35-year-old La Luz del Sol subdivision near Coors and Montano NW, also was the signature community in the parade.

It was chosen in part because it is on the West Side and “it was a West Side parade” with many homes in that part of town, Garcia said.

“It’s the West Side, but it’s infill on the West Side and that’s really not something you see very often,” Ashcraft said.

With few opportunities for big new developments other than Mesa del Sol and the proposed 13,700-acre Santolina master-planned community on the West Mesa south of Interstate 40, “infill is the next new,” Garcia said. “It’s not a bad thing. It’s just a change in focus.”

The 2,146-square-foot, three-bedroom model that won so many awards is the first home to be built in Las Ventanas’ latest project. It is priced at $349,900.

Most homebuilders upgrade their models with extras that add thousands to the base price, but at Ventana de la Luz, what you see is what you get.

Ventana de la Luz
Plan C
STANDARD FEATURES: Granite kitchen and bathroom countertops, insulated and finished garage, Pella vinyl windows, stainless-steel Bosch appliances, brushed nickel light fixtures, tankless water heater, blown-in insulation, California Closets system in master suite
HEATING/COOLING: Forced air furnace/refrigerated cooling
INSULATION VALUES: R-38 in the ceiling, R-23 in the walls

“This is how we build our houses,” said builder T. Scott Ashcraft. “It’s very different how we go about our presentation.”

Las Ventanas specializes in designing smaller homes with high-end finishes and silver certification from Build Green New Mexico. The company’s floor plans are open and emphasize usable space rather than formal areas.

Although it is much smaller than many new homes in that price range, the house has an open, airy feeling with 12-foot ceilings in the main living area and plenty of natural light.

“I think it’s important you design a house where people use all the space,” Ashcraft said. “It feels and lives like a much bigger home.”

The large entry leads directly into the great room – one big open space in which the kitchen, dining area and family room have a view of the Sandia Mountains and a bit of the bosque through 12-by-8-foot sliding glass doors. Roof windows in the entry and kitchen – built with completely insulated clear glass – add to the effect.

The kitchen has handsome solid-wood cabinets that stretch 42 inches high, a large pantry and a wide counter with enough space for five stools.

Sliding glass doors in the master bedroom open onto a covered back porch with a beautiful view of the Sandia Mountains. (Adolphe Pierre-Louis/Albuquerque Journal)

In the master bedroom, another set of 12-by-8-foot sliding glass doors open onto a covered back porch and the same beautiful view. The master bathroom has another roof window and a clerestory window over the garden tub.

A second full bathroom and a secondary bedroom sit near the front of the house.

A special feature of the home is a casita with a separate entrance off the gated front courtyard. The casita has the same wood-look tile flooring, a full bathroom and a walk-in closet.

“It works great as an actual casita or guest quarters,” said Ashcraft. “Or it is great as a home office or art studio.”

Among the thoughtful design elements Las Ventanas adds to its homes are aging-in-place features. In this model, for example, all the doorways are extra wide, there is no curb to step over into the showers and the toilet is a taller-than-standard model.

“It’s the little things, but they all add up,” Ashcraft said.

Las Ventanas is building a second model, the Plan B, which has three bedrooms and two bathrooms in 2,125 square feet. It does not have a casita but has an extra garden patio off the great room and sells for $339,900.

Skylights and an open floor plan are among the selling points of this $349,900 model home near Coors and
Montaño NW. (Adolphe Pierre-Louis/Albuquerque Journal)

The company waited to hear comments from Parade of Homes visitors before finalizing the design for a third floor plan.

“People wanted something even smaller than this,” Ashcraft said, adding that the next floor plan will be a two-bedroom, two-bathroom home.

All 30 lots are on one north-south street so all enjoy the same views. Wide swaths of open space with native landscaping across the street from the homes and on the slope below them behind give the outdoors the same open feeling as the inside of the model home.

As part of the gated La Luz del Sol community designed by noted architect Antoine Predock, homeowners have access to a pool and pay $160 a month in homeowners’ association fees.
