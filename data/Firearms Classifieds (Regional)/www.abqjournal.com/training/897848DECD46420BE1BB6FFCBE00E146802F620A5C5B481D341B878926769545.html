APD investigating a suspicious death at Coors and Bluewater NW

Authorities investigate a suspicious death that occurred near Coors and Bluewater NW

By Elise Kaplan / Journal Staff Writer
Published: Monday, October 12th, 2015 at 5:40pm
Updated: Monday, October 12th, 2015 at 6:38pm
.......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... ..........

A dead man was found in the backyard of a vacant house in northwest Albuquerque Monday afternoon, according to police.

Homicide detectives responded to the house at Coors and Bluewater NW around 4 p.m. when a woman called police to report the body, visible through a run-down fence, said officer Simon Drobik, a spokesman for the Albuquerque Police Department.

“The cause of death will always be confirmed by the Office of the Medical Investigator,” he said. “We were called due to the suspicious nature of the death. Then they believed there was enough evidence there to make it a homicide callout.”

Drobik did not know how the man died, his name or his age.

The body, now covered by a plastic sheet, could be seen lying near debris in the backyard of the boarded up house. The house was found to be an uninhabitable building last March, according to a notice posted on the door.

Alejandro Molina lives next door to the house and said he’s called the police to report vandalism and other disturbances four times over the past seven years. He said he’s seen a variety of people sleeping on the front porch or in the back yard.

“Some people live for several months then they go,” he said. “Then there’s some other people that come around.”

He said he didn’t hear anything before the police showed up.

-This is a developing story. More information will be provided as it becomes available.
