Police ID carjacking suspect on the loose

By Robert Browman / Of the Journal
Thursday, October 1st, 2015 at 4:09pm
.......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... .......... ..........

Oscar Anchondo, 38. (MDC)

Albuquerque police identified a suspect they slipped away from them Wednesday after an all-day effort to arrest him that included an officer opening fire.

Officer Tanner Tixier, a department spokesman, said police are searching for Oscar Anchondo, 38, after he fled from officers during a carjacking call and pointed a gun at an officer, who then fired at him.

Police followed Anchondo to a Northeast Albuquerque mobile home park, but after after an hours-long SWAT call, they found he had slipped away again.

Tixier said Anchondo is wanted in connection to numerous robberies that took place since September 22nd.

“Anchondo is considered to be armed and extremely dangerous,” Tixier said.

Wednesday’s incident began around 10 a.m. when officers were sent to Española and Grand NE after a security company gave them GPS data on a car that had carjacked, Tixier said.

When officers arrived at the scene, they took two suspects into custody, but a third suspect jumped into a stolen vehicle and fled, chief Gorden Eden said Wednesday.

“As the suspect fled the scene, he pointed the gun at one of our officers, who shot at the suspect an unknown number of times,” Eden said.

Officers followed Anchondo to a mobile home park at San Pedro and Karlson where they thought they had him cornered. They called in the SWAT team, but after more than eight hours, they realized Anchondo had escaped.

“Oscar Anchondo is wanted on an outstanding felony warrant for a probation violation, stemming from an armed robbery charge,” Tixier said. “He is also the suspect in three armed carjackings and three armed robberies to several businesses.”

Tixier said the car Anchondo was driving on Wednesday was taken during an armed carjacking on Tuesday.

He said police will not be releasing more information about the shooting at this time.

“As is standard with all officer-involved shootings, we will not be releasing any further information about the incident, lapel video, or officer’s name until he has had a chance to be interviewed,” he said.

Tixier asked anyone with information about Anchondo’s whereabouts to call 242-COPS.

— This is a developing story. Additional information will be added as it becomes available.
