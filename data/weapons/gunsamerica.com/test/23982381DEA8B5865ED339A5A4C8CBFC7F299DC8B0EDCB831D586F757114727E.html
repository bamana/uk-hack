New Six-Guns from Cimarron–SHOT Show 2015

by Sam Trisler on January 26, 2015

Related Tags: Buzz, Feature Articles, Guns

Send to Kindle

  • Twitter
  • Google+

  • Reddit

Cimarron is one of the leaders in the reproduction of 19th century firearms. Even if they don’t manufacture the guns themselves, they refine them. I stopped by their booth at SHOT Show to see what new revolvers they had.Â  Of course new is a relative term.Â  Both of the new revolvers they showcased are based on the Colt 1873. So new old revolvers.

The first one the gentleman, I say gentleman because he was dressed in late 19th century attire, pulled out of the case was a beauty. Take a look at the photos to see what I mean.Â  This was a very nicely done, laser engraved Peace Maker. The bluing was dark and rich and the French grey style finish helped the engraving pop. The MSRP was right at $500. So new technology and old technology, and reasonably priced, too.

The other revolver I got a chance to look at was a prototype.Â  I hope they make this one so I can buy one. It has a few things that set it apart from other Peace Maker style revolvers.Â  First it has an octagonal barrel that just looks cool.Â  It also has a short stroke hammer for faster cocking, a popular custom style upgrade a lot of SASS shooters do to their competition guns.Â  The other big difference is the grip.Â  This, as-yet-unnamed revolver has the grip frame of a Colt Walker.Â  This is a grip for shooters with big hands.Â  See the photo below for a comparison.
