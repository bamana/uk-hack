FFL TRANSFERS:

$75.00 ($50 intake fee + $25 State DROS fee) + State Sales Tax (if applicable)*

Additional firearms (on the same DROS form):
$40 each - no additional DROS fee

Handguns are restricted to 1 every 30 days per CA DOJ requirement; however, all transactions done at the same time can be done on one DROS form for only one DROS fee.

FFL TRANSFER REQUEST INSTRUCTIONS:

• Complete your transaction with the seller/ FFL. If there is a question of whether the firearm you are purchasing is legal in the State, please refer to the CA DOJ Roster of Handguns (﻿http://certguns.doj.ca.gov/﻿) or contact us with questions.

• Email us a copy of your order confirmation at sjguntrader@gmail.com. If we are not already on file with the selling FFL, please include contact information and instructions and we will contact them with all necessary paperwork required for shipment.  Please include the following information in your email: Your full name, contact information, Firearm details (Make/ Model), the Seller's info and/or FFL info, and contact info for shipping FFL.

• Once your firearm arrives, we will contact you to schedule a time to come in and start the paperwork. Please note: We cannot begin paperwork on any gun that is not physically in our shop. You must wait until your gun arrives to schedule a time to come in and start the background check process.

• We will accept firearms transferred from private party individuals for long guns only. The seller must include a copy of their Driver's license or identification along with a receipt showing the sale price.  Please note: We do NOT provide a copy of our FFL to non-FFL holders per ATF requirements.  We will provide the information necessary for the seller to perform an FFL e-z check as proof of our valid licensing.  This is sufficient to meet the shipping requirements for all parcel carriers.  If your seller will not comply with this requirement, they must ship through an authorized FFL dealer.

• PLEASE NOTE: It is the responsibility of the Buyer to know and comply with all California laws.  Firearms transferred into California must be CA Compliant, no exceptions. Firearms that do not comply with state laws will be returned to the sender at the Buyer's expense.

Transfer and DROS fees are NOT REFUNDABLE on a DENIED DROS and additional fees for the return of the firearm may apply.


CALIFORNIA BOARD OF EQUALIZATION SALES TAX:

* If a firearm is being received from out of state, CA Sales Tax must be collected on the entire purchase price, including shipping fees, per BOE requirements.  A detailed invoice must be provided with the shipment so fees can be calculated accordingly.

Firearms purchased within the state of California must be accompanied by a detailed invoice indicating that tax has already been collected by the seller.  Invoice must include the purchaser's name, firearm information (make, model serial #) and seller details.

If no invoice is provided, CA Sales Tax will be determined by the "Fair Market Value" of the firearm.  We still require an invoice showing seller's information, Buyer's information and firearm make/ model/ serial number information.  No paperwork will be done on a firearm that does not have this information on file.

PRIVATE PARTY TRANSFERS:

$35.00 ($10.00 Dealer Fee + $25 State DROS fee)

Additional private party transfer firearms on the same DROS form are $10.00 each with no additional DROS fee.

A private party transfer requires both the Buyer and Seller to be present at the time of the transaction.  Both must be California residents and have current identification.

Please be sure that all firearms and magazines are unloaded and properly secured before bringing them in to the shop.

FIREARM SAFETY CERTIFICATE (FSC):

**Effective January 1st, 2015**

We offer the FSC test at our location.  Cost is $25 - $15 paid to the State and $10 dealer fee.

This is a 30 question test and is required in order to purchase any handgun or long gun in the state of California as of January 1, 2015.  Please see our "Buying a Gun" link for acceptable exemptions to this requirement.

The FSC Study guide can be found here:  http://oag.ca.gov/sites/all/files/agweb/pdfs/firearms/forms/hscsg.pdf?

If you have questions about the FSC Program, their FAQ can be found here: http://oag.ca.gov/firearms/fscfaqs
